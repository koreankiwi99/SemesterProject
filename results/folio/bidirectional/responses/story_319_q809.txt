Premises: No baked sweets are spicy.
All cupcakes are baked sweets.
All mala hotpots are spicy.
All products from Baked by Melissa are cupcakes.
Dried Thai chilies are spicy or mala hotpots or not baked sweets.
Conclusion: Dried Thai chilies are cupcakes and products of Baked by Melissa.

-- 2. All cupcakes are baked sweets.

-- 4. All products from Baked by Melissa are cupcakes.

-- 5. Dried Thai chilies are spicy or mala hotpots or not baked sweets.
axiom h5 : Spicy DriedThaiChilies ∨ MalaHotpot DriedThaiChilies ∨ ¬ BakedSweet DriedThaiChilies

-- Conclusion to analyze:
-- Dried Thai chilies are cupcakes and products of Baked by Melissa.
theorem goal : Cupcake DriedThaiChilies ∧ ProductOfBBM DriedThaiChilies := by
  -- Attempt to derive the conclusion from the given premises.
  -- However, the premises do not provide any way to conclude that Dried Thai chilies
  -- are cupcakes or products of Baked by Melissa.
  -- We analyze the given disjunction but none of its cases yield the desired conclusion.
  have disj := h5
  cases disj with
  | inl hSpicy =>
    -- hSpicy : Spicy DriedThaiChilies
    -- From being spicy we cannot conclude cupcake or product of BBM.
    sorry
  | inr rest =>
    cases rest with
    | inl hMala =>
      -- hMala : MalaHotpot DriedThaiChilies
      -- Then Spicy DriedThaiChilies by h3, but still no link to cupcake or BBM product.
      have hSpicy : Spicy DriedThaiChilies := h3 DriedThaiChilies hMala
      sorry
    | inr hNotBaked =>
      -- hNotBaked : ¬ BakedSweet DriedThaiChilies
      -- This also does not imply cupcake or BBM product.
