Premises: People in this club who perform in school talent shows often attend and are very engaged with school events.
People in this club either perform in school talent shows often or are inactive and disinterested community members.
People in this club who chaperone high school dances are not students who attend the school.
All people in this club who are inactive and disinterested members of their community chaperone high school dances.
All young children and teenagers in this club who wish to further their academic careers and educational opportunities are students who attend the school. 
Bonnie is in this club and she either both attends and is very engaged with school events and is a student who attends the school or is not someone who both attends and is very engaged with school events and is not a student who attends the school.
Conclusion: Bonnie performs in school talent shows often.

-- Premises
axiom premise1 : ∀ x : Person, Club x ∧ PerformOften x → AttendEngaged x
axiom premise2 : ∀ x : Person, Club x → PerformOften x ∨ InactiveDisinterested x
axiom premise3 : ∀ x : Person, Club x ∧ ChaperoneDances x → ¬ StudentAttendee x
axiom premise4 : ∀ x : Person, Club x ∧ InactiveDisinterested x → ChaperoneDances x
axiom premise5 : ∀ x : Person, Club x ∧ YoungOrTeen x ∧ WishesAcademic x → StudentAttendee x

-- Bonnie-specific information:
-- Interpreting: Bonnie is in this club and she either
-- (attends & is very engaged and is a student) or
-- (is not someone who attends & is very engaged and is not a student)
-- i.e., (AttendEngaged ∧ StudentAttendee) ∨ (¬AttendEngaged ∧ ¬StudentAttendee)
axiom premise6 :
  Club Bonnie ∧
  ((AttendEngaged Bonnie ∧ StudentAttendee Bonnie) ∨
   ((¬ AttendEngaged Bonnie) ∧ (¬ StudentAttendee Bonnie)))

-- Goal
theorem conclusion : PerformOften Bonnie := by
  have hClub : Club Bonnie := And.left premise6
  have hDisj :
      (AttendEngaged Bonnie ∧ StudentAttendee Bonnie) ∨
      ((¬ AttendEngaged Bonnie) ∧ (¬ StudentAttendee Bonnie)) :=
    And.right premise6
  cases hDisj with
  | inl h1 =>
    have hStud : StudentAttendee Bonnie := And.right h1
    -- Show Inactive is impossible; then use premise2 to conclude Perform
    have notInactive : ¬ InactiveDisinterested Bonnie := by
      intro hInact
      have hChap : ChaperoneDances Bonnie := premise4 Bonnie (And.intro hClub hInact)
      have hNotStud : ¬ StudentAttendee Bonnie := premise3 Bonnie (And.intro hClub hChap)
      exact hNotStud hStud
    have hOr : PerformOften Bonnie ∨ InactiveDisinterested Bonnie := premise2 Bonnie hClub
    cases hOr with
    | inl hPerf => exact hPerf
    | inr hInact => exact (False.elim (notInactive hInact))
  | inr h2 =>
    -- Here we have ¬AttendEngaged Bonnie and ¬StudentAttendee Bonnie.
    -- From the given premises, this branch does not yield PerformOften Bonnie.
    sorry
