Premises: If people spill a lot of food on their clothing, then they are not notably tidy.
All clumsy foodies who go out frequently to find new food restaurants to try are people who spill a lot of food on their clothing.
If people are cleanly, then they are notably tidy.
If people value order and spotlessness, then they are cleanly.
All people whose families prioritize order and spotlessness themselves value order and spotlessness.
Peter is either a person who spills a lot of food on his clothing and is cleanly, or a person who does not spill a lot of food on his clothing and is not cleanly
Conclusion: Peter is either a  clumsy foodie who goes out frequently to find new food restaurants to try or he is someone whose family prioritizes order and spotlessness.

-- Premises:
axiom h1 : ∀ x : Person, spills x → ¬ tidy x
axiom h2 : ∀ x : Person, clumsyFoodie x → spills x
axiom h3 : ∀ x : Person, cleanly x → tidy x
axiom h4 : ∀ x : Person, valueOrder x → cleanly x
axiom h5 : ∀ x : Person, famPrior x → valueOrder x
axiom h6 : (spills Peter ∧ cleanly Peter) ∨ (¬ spills Peter ∧ ¬ cleanly Peter)
axiom h7 : clumsyFoodie Peter ∨ famPrior Peter

-- Target theorem (interpreting the query as "Peter is notably tidy"):
theorem peter_tidy : tidy Peter :=
by
  -- First, derive that Peter is cleanly.
  have hc : cleanly Peter :=
    Or.elim h7
      (fun hcf => 
        -- From clumsyFoodie Peter, get spills Peter
        let hs : spills Peter := h2 Peter hcf
        -- Use the disjunction about Peter to force cleanly Peter
        Or.elim h6
          (fun hA => hA.right)
          (fun hB =>
            -- hB: ¬spills Peter ∧ ¬cleanly Peter, contradicts hs
            False.elim (hB.left hs)))
      (fun hfam =>
        -- From family prioritizes order → value order → cleanly
        h4 Peter (h5 Peter hfam))
  -- From cleanly → tidy, conclude tidy Peter
  exact h3 Peter hc
