Story ID: 386
Example ID: 1033
Premises: If something is a deadly disease, then it comes with a low survival rate. 
Severe cancers are deadly diseases.
Bile duct cancer is a severe form cancer. 
All Cholangiocarcinoma is bile duct cancer.
Mild flu comes with a low survival rate.
Colorectal cancer is not both a bile duct cancer and with a low survival rate.
Conclusion: If colorectal cancer is a kind of bile duct cancer or a form of Cholangiocarcinoma, then colorectal cancer is a kind of bile duct cancer and a kind of mild flu.

namespace Reasoning

constant Disease : Type

constant Deadly : Disease → Prop

constant Severe : Disease → Prop

axiom A2 : ∀ x : Disease, Severe x → Deadly x
axiom A3 : ∀ x : Disease, BileDuct x → Severe x

axiom A5 : ∀ x : Disease, MildFlu x → LowSurvival x
axiom A6 : ¬ (BileDuct ColorectalCancer ∧ LowSurvival ColorectalCancer)

theorem target :
  (BileDuct ColorectalCancer ∨ Cholangiocarcinoma ColorectalCancer) →
  (BileDuct ColorectalCancer ∧ MildFlu ColorectalCancer) := by
  have not_bd : ¬ BileDuct ColorectalCancer := by
    intro hbd
    have hsev : Severe ColorectalCancer := A3 ColorectalCancer hbd
    have hded : Deadly ColorectalCancer := A2 ColorectalCancer hsev
    have hlow : LowSurvival ColorectalCancer := A1 ColorectalCancer hded
    exact A6 ⟨hbd, hlow⟩
  intro h
  cases h with
  | inl hbd => exact False.elim (not_bd hbd)
  | inr hch =>
      have hbd : BileDuct ColorectalCancer := A4 ColorectalCancer hch
      exact False.elim (not_bd hbd)

end Reasoning
