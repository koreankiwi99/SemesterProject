Example ID: 1269
Premises: No one nice to animals is also mean to animals.
Some grumpy people are mean to animals.
All animal lovers are nice to animals.
All pet owners love animals.
Tom is a pet owner.
Conclusion: Tom is not both a grumpy person and mean to animals.

==================================================
Iteration 1
==================================================
<lean>
constant Person : Type

constant Nice : Person → Prop
constant Mean : Person → Prop
constant Grumpy : Person → Prop
constant PetOwner : Person → Prop
constant LovesAnimals : Person → Prop

constant Tom : Person

-- Premises
axiom noNiceMean : ∀ x : Person, Nice x → ¬ Mean x
axiom someGrumpyMean : ∃ x : Person, Grumpy x ∧ Mean x
axiom loversNice : ∀ x : Person, LovesAnimals x → Nice x
axiom ownersLovers : ∀ x : Person, PetOwner x → LovesAnimals x
axiom tomOwner : PetOwner Tom

-- Conclusion: Tom is not both grumpy and mean to animals.
theorem conclusion : ¬ (Grumpy Tom ∧ Mean Tom) := by
  have hL : LovesAnimals Tom := ownersLovers Tom tomOwner
  have hN : Nice Tom := loversNice Tom hL
  have hNM : ¬ Mean Tom := noNiceMean Tom hN
  intro h
  have hm : Mean Tom := And.right h
  exact hNM hm
</lean>
