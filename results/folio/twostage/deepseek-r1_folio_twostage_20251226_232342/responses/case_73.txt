We are dealing with objects (registration plates or perhaps cars?) and their properties.
 However, note that the premises talk about registration plates and then specifically about Joe's and Tom's plates.

   - A predicate `BeginsWith34 : Plate → Prop` meaning that the plate number begins with 34.
   - We also need a way to talk about beginning with 35: `BeginsWith35 : Plate → Prop`.

 Premises:
   1. All vehicle registration plates in Istanbul begin with 34.
      ∀ p : Plate, FromIstanbul p → BeginsWith34 p

   2. Plates that do not begin with 34 are not from Istanbul.
      ∀ p : Plate, ¬ BeginsWith34 p → ¬ FromIstanbul p

   3. Joe's vehicle registration plate is from Istanbul.
      FromIstanbul JoesPlate

   4. Tom's license plate begins with 35.
      BeginsWith35 TomsPlate

   5. If a license plate begins with 35, then it does not begin with 34.
      ∀ p : Plate, BeginsWith35 p → ¬ BeginsWith34 p

 Conclusion: Joe's license plate begins with the number 34.
   BeginsWith34 JoesPlate

 We'll define:
   - `Plate` as a type.
   - Constants `JoesPlate` and `TomsPlate` of type `Plate`.
   - Predicates `FromIstanbul`, `BeginsWith34`, `BeginsWith35`.

 Let's write the axioms accordingly.

Here's the Lean 4 formalization based on the given premises and conclusion. The solution uses plates as entities and defines predicates for their properties:

axiom TomsPlate : Plate
axiom FromIstanbul : Plate → Prop

</lean>

Key points:
1. Introduced `Plate` as the base type
2. Created specific plates for Joe and Tom
3. Defined three predicates:
   - Istanbul origin (`FromIstanbul`)
   - 34-prefix (`BeginsWith34`)

4. Translated all 5 premises as axioms
5. Formalized the conclusion as a theorem with `sorry`

Note: The second premise (axiom A2) is logically equivalent to the contrapositive of the first premise in classical logic, but is included since it was explicitly stated.
