Premises: Everything is either outside the solar system or in the solar system. 
Nothing outside the solar system has the Sun as its star.
Everything in the solar system is gravitationally bound by the Sun.
No planets gravitationally bound by the Sun are rogue planets. 
All orphan planets are rogue planets.
If PSO J318.5−22 is not both a rogue planet and a planet gravitationally bound by the Sun, then it is a rogue planet.
Conclusion: PSO J318.5−22 is an orphan planet.

STAGE 1: Natural language reasoning

Let p denote PSO J318.5−22.

- From “All orphan planets are rogue planets” we formalize: ∀x, Orphan(x) → Rogue(x).
- From “PSO J318.5−22 is an orphan planet” we have Orphan(p).
- By universal instantiation on the first statement with x = p, we get Orphan(p) → Rogue(p).
- By modus ponens with Orphan(p), we conclude Rogue(p).

Optional consistency check (not needed for the main conclusion):
- “No planets gravitationally bound by the Sun are rogue planets” is ∀x, (Planet(x) ∧ BoundBySun(x)) → ¬Rogue(x).
- For x = p, (Planet(p) ∧ BoundBySun(p)) → ¬Rogue(p). Taking the contrapositive yields Rogue(p) → ¬(Planet(p) ∧ BoundBySun(p)).
- Since we have Rogue(p), we infer ¬(Planet(p) ∧ BoundBySun(p)), which makes the antecedent of the conditional “If p is not both a rogue planet and a planet gravitationally bound by the Sun, then it is a rogue planet” true, and thus by modus ponens reconfirm Rogue(p).

Therefore, PSO J318.5−22 is a rogue planet.
