2. John has a presentation: PrepareWell John → ReadyForMeeting John
3. John is certain: CloseToLunchtime ∨ ¬ReadyForMeeting John
4. Office understanding: ∀x, Diligent x → PrepareWell x

The goal is to prove ¬(¬CoworkersHungry → Diligent John), equivalent to ¬CoworkersHungry ∧ ¬Diligent John. However:

- No axiom forces ¬CoworkersHungry to be true.

- John's knowledge and certainty are beliefs, not facts, so they don't establish truth values.
- Without ¬CoworkersHungry being derivable, the conjunction cannot be proven.

Thus, the negation cannot be proven from the premises.
