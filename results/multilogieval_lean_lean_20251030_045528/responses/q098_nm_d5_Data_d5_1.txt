Context:
In a zoo, lions and tigers are carnivores. Normally, carnivores in the zoo are fed raw meat. If an animal has a mane and roars loudly, visitors find it majestic. If a big cat has stripes and hunts at night, visitors are intrigued. Visitors find either an animal with a mane majestic or a big cat that hunts at night intriguing, or both. If the lion is majestic, then the tiger is intriguing. If the tiger is fed raw meat, then the lion is majestic.

Question:
Can we conclude if the lion is not majestic then the tiger is not fed raw meat?

axiom Mane : Animal → Prop

axiom Majestic : Animal → Prop

-- Lions and tigers are carnivores
axiom lion_is_carnivore : Carnivore lion
axiom tiger_is_carnivore : Carnivore tiger

-- Normally, carnivores are fed raw meat
axiom carnivores_fed_raw_meat : ∀ a : Animal, Carnivore a → FedRawMeat a

-- If an animal has a mane and roars loudly, it is majestic
axiom mane_and_roar_implies_majestic : ∀ a : Animal, Mane a ∧ RoarsLoudly a → Majestic a

-- If a big cat has stripes and hunts at night, visitors are intrigued
axiom bigcat_stripes_night_implies_intrigued : ∀ a : Animal, BigCat a ∧ Stripes a ∧ HuntsAtNight a → Intrigued a

-- Optional disjunctive visitor rule (not needed for the conclusion)
axiom visitors_rule : ∀ a : Animal, (Mane a → Majestic a) ∨ (BigCat a ∧ HuntsAtNight a → Intrigued a)

-- If the lion is majestic, then the tiger is intrigued
axiom lion_majestic_implies_tiger_intrigued : Majestic lion → Intrigued tiger

-- If the tiger is fed raw meat, then the lion is majestic
axiom tiger_fed_implies_lion_majestic : FedRawMeat tiger → Majestic lion

-- Conclusion: If the lion is not majestic, then the tiger is not fed raw meat
theorem lion_not_majestic_implies_tiger_not_fed :
  ¬ Majestic lion → ¬ FedRawMeat tiger := by
  intro hNotMaj
  intro hFedTiger
  have hMajLion : Majestic lion := tiger_fed_implies_lion_majestic hFedTiger
  exact hNotMaj hMajLion
