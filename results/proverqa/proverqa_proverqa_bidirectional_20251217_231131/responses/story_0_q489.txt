Premises: Simba feels invincible. If Simba either learns from mistakes or listens to others (but not both), then he values wisdom. Yummers feels invincible. Simba does not develop caution. Simba either follows the advice of his elders or exhibits impulsive behavior, but not both. Simba seeks adventure. Simba respects experience. If Simba avoids reckless behavior, then he values wisdom. Rhys respects experience. Every lion who feels invincible either asserts dominance or has confidence (or both). Every lion who takes risks either develops caution or learns from mistakes (or both). For all lions, if they have confidence and seek adventure, then they take risks. If Simba values wisdom and respects experience, then he follows the advice of his elders. Any lion that respects tradition or values prudence follows the advice of their elders. Simba does not listen to others. Simba does not assert dominance.
Conclusion: Simba will not face danger.

axiom p13 : (ValuesWisdom Simba ∧ RespectsExperience Simba) → FollowsElders Simba

-- Conclusion to prove: Simba will not face danger.
theorem conclusion : ¬ FacesDanger Simba := by
  -- From invincibility and premise p10, Simba asserts dominance or has confidence.
  have hAC : AssertsDominance Simba ∨ HasConfidence Simba := p10 _ p1
  -- Using ¬AssertsDominance, deduce HasConfidence.
  have hConf : HasConfidence Simba := Or.resolve_left hAC p16
  -- With confidence and seeking adventure, Simba takes risks.
  have hRisk : TakesRisks Simba := p12 _ ⟨hConf, p6⟩
  -- From taking risks, Simba develops caution or learns from mistakes.
  have hC_or_L : DevelopsCaution Simba ∨ LearnsFromMistakes Simba := p11 _ hRisk
  -- But Simba does not develop caution, hence he learns from mistakes.
  have hLearn : LearnsFromMistakes Simba := Or.resolve_left hC_or_L p4
  -- Use the XOR-to-wisdom premise p2 together with ¬ListensToOthers.
  have hWisdom : ValuesWisdom Simba := by
    apply p2
    refine And.intro ?h1 ?h2
    · exact Or.inl hLearn
    · intro hBoth
      have : ListensToOthers Simba := And.right hBoth
      exact p15 this
  -- With wisdom and respecting experience, Simba follows elders.
  have hFollow : FollowsElders Simba := p13 ⟨hWisdom, p7⟩
  -- From the XOR about following elders vs impulsive, deduce not impulsive.
  have hNotImp : ¬ ImpulsiveBehavior Simba := by
    have hNotBoth : ¬ (FollowsElders Simba ∧ ImpulsiveBehavior Simba) := And.right p5
    intro hImp
    exact hNotBoth ⟨hFollow, hImp⟩
  -- At this point, we lack any premise connecting FacesDanger to the established facts.
  -- Therefore, the desired conclusion cannot be derived from the given premises.
  sorry
