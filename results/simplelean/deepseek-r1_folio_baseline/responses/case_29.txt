The reasoning involves analyzing the given context and determining the truth value of the statement: "If Marvin is not from Mars, then Marvin is a human."

### Key Steps:
1. **Formalize Context**:
   - All aliens are extraterrestrials: `∀x, Alien x → Extraterrestrial x`
   - If from Mars, then alien: `∀x, FromMars x → Alien x`
   - No extraterrestrials are human: `∀x, Extraterrestrial x → ¬Human x`
   - Highly intelligent beings from Earth are human: `∀x, (HighlyIntelligent x ∧ FromEarth x) → Human x`
   - Marvin is highly intelligent: `HighlyIntelligent Marvin`
   - Marvin is either from both Earth and Mars or from neither: `(FromEarth Marvin ∧ FromMars Marvin) ∨ (¬FromEarth Marvin ∧ ¬FromMars Marvin)`
   - If not from Earth, then extraterrestrial: `¬FromEarth Marvin → Extraterrestrial Marvin`

2. **Analyze Cases**:
   - **Case 1**: Marvin is from both Earth and Mars.
     - From Earth: By axiom 4, Marvin is human (`Human Marvin`).
     - From Mars: By axioms 2 and 1, Marvin is extraterrestrial, so not human (`¬Human Marvin`). Contradiction. Thus, Case 1 is impossible.
   - **Case 2**: Marvin is from neither Earth nor Mars (only remaining possibility).
     - Not from Earth: By axiom 7, Marvin is extraterrestrial.
     - By axiom 3, Marvin is not human (`¬Human Marvin`).
     - Not from Mars (`¬FromMars Marvin`) is true, but Marvin is not human. Thus, the implication `¬FromMars Marvin → Human Marvin` is false (antecedent true, consequent false).

3. **Conclusion**:
   - The statement "If Marvin is not from Mars, then Marvin is a human" is false because in the only possible scenario (Case 2), Marvin is not from Mars but is also not human.
