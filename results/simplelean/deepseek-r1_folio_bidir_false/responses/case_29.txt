To solve this problem, we need to determine if the conclusion "If Marvin is not from Mars, then Marvin is a human" is false based on the given premises. We'll formalize the premises in Lean and attempt to prove the negation of the conclusion.

### Step 1: Formalize the Premises
- **Entities and Predicates**: We define a type `obj` for entities and predicates for properties like `Alien`, `Extraterrestrial`, `Human`, `FromMars`, `FromEarth`, and `HighlyIntelligent`.
- **Axioms**:
  1. All aliens are extraterrestrials: `∀x, Alien x → Extraterrestrial x`.
  2. If someone is from Mars, they are an alien: `∀x, FromMars x → Alien x`.
  3. No extraterrestrials are human: `∀x, Extraterrestrial x → ¬Human x`.
  4. All highly intelligent beings from Earth are humans: `∀x, (HighlyIntelligent x ∧ FromEarth x) → Human x`.
  5. Marvin is highly intelligent: `HighlyIntelligent Marvin`.
  6. Marvin is either from both Earth and Mars or from neither: `(FromEarth Marvin ∧ FromMars Marvin) ∨ (¬FromEarth Marvin ∧ ¬FromMars Marvin)`.
  7. If Marvin is not from Earth, he is an extraterrestrial: `¬FromEarth Marvin → Extraterrestrial Marvin`.

### Step 2: Prove the Conclusion is False
The conclusion is: `¬FromMars Marvin → Human Marvin`. We prove this is false by showing its negation: `¬FromMars Marvin ∧ ¬Human Marvin`.

#### Proof Strategy:
1. **Case Analysis**: Use axiom 6 to split into two cases:
   - **Case 1**: Marvin is from both Earth and Mars.
     - This leads to a contradiction: 
       - From Mars → Alien → Extraterrestrial → ¬Human (by axioms 2, 1, 3).
       - From Earth and highly intelligent → Human (by axiom 4).
     - Contradiction: Marvin cannot be both human and not human.
   - **Case 2**: Marvin is from neither Earth nor Mars.
     - Not from Earth → Extraterrestrial (axiom 7) → ¬Human (axiom 3).
     - Not from Mars is directly true.
2. **Result**: Case 1 is impossible, so Case 2 must hold, proving `¬FromMars Marvin ∧ ¬Human Marvin`.
